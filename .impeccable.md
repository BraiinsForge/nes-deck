## Design Context

### Users

The owner and nearby players use the Braiins Forge Deck as a dedicated NES
console. They interact at arm's length through its 1280x480 touchscreen and two
physical USB gamepads. The launcher's single job is to let them recognize and
start a game immediately.

### Brand Personality

Lean, direct, and retro-utilitarian. The interface should feel like a purpose-
built console menu, not a content catalog, marketing page, or AI-generated card
grid.

### Aesthetic Direction

Keep the existing native framebuffer presentation and strong game colors, but
remove explanatory and legal metadata from the launcher. Each game surface
shows its name and nothing else. Controls must remain obvious, touch targets
must remain generous, and the fixed landscape panel should be used deliberately
without decorative clutter.

### Design Principles

1. Game selection is the one primary task; titles are the only game metadata
   shown in the launcher.
2. Prefer immediate recognition and large touch targets over dense information.
3. Keep operational controls explicit, but do not repeat instructions or expose
   catalog/legal details that belong in repository documentation.
4. Preserve the Deck's distinctive retro palette while simplifying hierarchy
   and spacing.
5. Design for physical use: two-player gamepads, touchscreen launch, and clear
   state at a glance.
